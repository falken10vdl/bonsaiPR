## Why ancestry-merge is the right tool

#8228's *content* genuinely needs #7813's change — once `class profile` is removed, the target's `with profile(...)` calls would be undefined names. But #7813's change is small (one commit, one file) and its intent is orthogonal to #8228's feature. Merging #7813's commit into the #8228 branch:

1. Brings #7813's profiler swap + import + `class profile` removal into the branch (a normal 3-way merge; the three stage-block lines conflict and are resolved by hand).
2. Makes `1acce39867` an **ancestor** of #8228. In the build, the merge-base between the build tip (which contains #7813) and #8228 shifts up to include `1acce39867`, so #7813's changes are no longer "one side of a contest" — they are shared history. Only #8228's further edits remain visible → clean auto-merge.

`git merge -s ours` was rejected: it would keep the target's tree verbatim (old `profile(...)` form + reintroduced `class profile`), contradicting #7813 and leaving broken/duplicated profiling code.
